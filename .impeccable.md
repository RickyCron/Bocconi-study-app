## Design Context

### Users
Ricky — university student on semester abroad at Bocconi, Milan. Uses this app solo for exam prep across 5 courses with fixed exam dates in May 2026. Context: studying in cafés, dorms, commuting. High cognitive load, wants to quickly know what to work on next.

### Brand Personality
Focused, sharp, personal. Not an enterprise tool — a private study companion. Feels like a well-crafted note-taking app, not a SaaS dashboard. Quiet but capable.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme (warm near-black, not cold). Already established: `#0d0d0f` base, OKLCH muted palette, Geist font, grain overlay. Muted course accent colors (not neon). The visual language is already intentional and cohesive — preserve it.

### Design Principles
1. **Information density over decoration** — every element earns its place; Ricky is in study mode, not browsing
2. **Urgency is the hierarchy** — the highest-priority course must be immediately obvious on any given day
3. **Touch-first** — swipe interactions over click-heavy UIs; works on phone and laptop
4. **Interruptible motion** — animations must be fast and dismissible; never block the user
5. **Emil Kowalski motion** — ease-out-expo curves, velocity-weighted snaps, no bounce, only transform + opacity
